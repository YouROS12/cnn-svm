### Maximizing Acceptance Chances

**Before Submission**:

1. **Read Recent Papers** (last 2 years) in target journal
   - Understand writing style
   - Note common structure
   - Check reference counts

2. **Match Scope Perfectly**
   - Read journal aims & scope
   - Cite recent papers from journal (3-5)
   - Explain fit in cover letter

3. **Meet All Requirements**
   - Check submission guidelines (page limit, format)
   - Use journal template (if available)
   - Follow reference style precisely

4. **Quality Over Quantity**
   - Better to have 2-3 strong datasets than 5 weak ones
   - Better to have 5-6 relevant baselines than 10 random ones

**Cover Letter**:
- Summarize key contributions (3-4 bullet points)
- Explain why paper fits journal
- Suggest 3-5 reviewers (with emails, no conflicts)
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Mention previous submission history (if any)

## üìÖ Timeline & Deadlines

### Journal Submission Timeline

**Typical Journal Review Process**:

```
Week 0: Submission
Week 1-2: Editor screening (desk reject ~10-15%)
Week 3-8: Peer review (2-4 reviewers)
Week 9: Reviews back
Week 10-12: Major revision
Week 13-16: Second round review
Week 17: Minor revision or accept
Week 18-20: Final revisions
Week 21: Accepted
Week 22-30: Copyediting, proofs
Week 31-40: Published online
```

- Frontiers in Plant Science

**Slow Journals** (6-12 months total):

## üéì Response to Reviews

### Common Review Patterns

**Major Revision** (~40-50% of submissions):
- Address all comments point-by-point
- Add requested experiments
- Clarify writing
- Resubmit within 2-3 months

**Minor Revision** (~20-30% of submissions):
- Fix small issues
- Clarify text
- Add minor experiments
- Resubmit within 1 month

**Reject & Resubmit** (~10-15%):
- Major flaws identified
- Need substantial new experiments
- Consider as new submission

**Desk Reject** (~10-15%):
- Out of scope
- Low quality
- Poor formatting
